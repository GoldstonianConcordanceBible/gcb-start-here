Most people don’t struggle with the Bible because the Bible is unclear.

They struggle because they don’t have a **map**.

They jump:
- from proverb to prophecy
- from gospel to genealogies
- from command to comfort
…without a way to connect the pieces.

Here’s a map I use to keep Scripture unified and to keep myself honest:

## 1) Mirror
**Mirror** is where Scripture examines you.

Not your enemies. Not “society.” You.

Mirror questions:
- What is this exposing in me?
- What do I want to avoid?
- Where am I rationalizing?
- Where am I blaming?

Mirror keeps faith from becoming performance.

## 2) Water
**Water** is cleansing understanding.

Not just “what does this verse say?” but:
- what does the passage mean in context?
- what covenant stage is this?
- what theme is developing here?

Water protects you from using verses like weapons.

Water makes you patient with the text.

## 3) Fire
**Fire** is applied obedience.

Not hot emotion. **Refined action.**

Fire questions:
- What must change in my choices?
- What must change in my speech?
- What must change in my relationships?
- What must change in my stewardship?

Fire turns theology into fruit.

## Why this framework matters (especially online)
Online religion often collapses into two extremes:
- emotion without accountability
- argument without holiness

Mirror → Water → Fire resists both.

It says:
- Mirror: “I will not lie to myself.”
- Water: “I will not twist the text.”
- Fire: “I will not refuse to obey.”

## A practical way to use this on any chapter
Try this simple method:

1. **Mirror (3 minutes):** write one sentence: “This confronts me about ______.”
2. **Water (7 minutes):** summarize the passage in your own words (no commentary yet).
3. **Fire (3 minutes):** write one action step you will do in the next 24 hours.

Small. Specific. Real.
