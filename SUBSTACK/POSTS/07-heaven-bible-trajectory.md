When people say “heaven,” they often imagine:
- floating clouds
- endless distance from earth
- a disembodied spiritual waiting room

But the Bible’s direction is more grounded and more powerful:

**God’s plan is not to abandon creation.**  
God’s plan is to **restore creation**.

## The Bible speaks about heaven in layers
If you want to read “heaven” biblically, track these layers:

### Layer 1: God’s dwelling / God’s throne
Heaven is described as the realm of God’s authority—where His will is perfectly done.

### Layer 2: The meeting place between realms
Scripture repeatedly shows heaven and earth interacting:
- visions
- angels
- the temple pattern (earth as a copy/shadow of a greater reality)

### Layer 3: The promised future (new creation)
This is the part many people miss:

The Bible’s final picture is not escape.  
It is **new heavens and new earth**—a restored creation with God dwelling among His people.

That’s why the end of the Bible feels like the beginning of the Bible:
- God with humanity
- creation healed
- evil judged
- worship and work reunited

## Why this changes your daily faith
If your goal is “escape earth,” you may treat this world like it doesn’t matter.

But if the biblical goal is “restoration,” you start to care about:
- holiness
- justice
- stewardship
- truth
- family
- work
- integrity

Not because you’re saving the world.

But because you’re practicing the future.

## A simple reading principle
When you see “heaven” in Scripture, ask:

Is this describing:
- God’s authority?
- God’s presence?
- God’s future restoration?

Those are connected, but not identical.
