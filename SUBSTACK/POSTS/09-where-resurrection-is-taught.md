Some people treat resurrection like it’s only about one miracle weekend.

But the Bible treats resurrection as something bigger:

**God’s answer to death itself.**

## Resurrection is woven through the storyline
You can see resurrection logic across Scripture in at least four ways:

### 1) Pattern: life out of “death” moments
God repeatedly brings life where things look finished:
- barren wombs producing covenant heirs
- exiles returning home
- nations judged and restored

### 2) Promise: God will not abandon His people
Hope grows in Scripture as God reveals:
- death is not the final word
- the faithful will not be discarded

### 3) Fulfillment: Jesus rises as firstfruits
The resurrection is not just proof.

It’s **inauguration**:
- the new creation begins in Him
- the Kingdom is announced with power
- the future arrives early

### 4) Destination: resurrection hope shapes ethics
If resurrection is real, then:
- holiness matters
- suffering is not meaningless
- obedience is not wasted
- faithfulness outlives the grave

## Why this matters for daily life
Without resurrection hope:
- faith becomes survival
- discipleship becomes therapy
- morality becomes preference

With resurrection hope:
- suffering gains meaning
- obedience gains endurance
- worship gains weight
