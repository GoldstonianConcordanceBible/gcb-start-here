If you want to understand the cross, do not rush past Exodus.

God teaches salvation in patterns.

And Exodus is one of the clearest patterns of deliverance in all Scripture.

## What Exodus teaches
Exodus is not merely ancient history.

It is theological architecture:
- bondage is real
- deliverance is initiated by God
- judgment confronts evil
- a substitute is provided
- a people are formed
- a covenant is given
- worship is restored

## Passover is not a random ritual
Passover teaches:
- redemption has a cost
- deliverance is personal
- protection is not earned by strength
- the substitute stands in the place of the threatened

Passover is a lived sermon.

God puts salvation into a meal so it can’t be forgotten.

## Why this points to Jesus
The Gospels do not invent meaning out of thin air.

They reveal fulfillment:
- the pattern becomes a person
- the shadow becomes substance
- the rescue becomes final

This is why Scripture is unified:

God doesn’t change His character.

He expands His revelation.

## A practical takeaway
When you read Exodus, don’t ask, “What does this have to do with me?”

Ask:
- What does this teach me about God?
- What does this teach me about salvation?
- What does this teach me about worship and obedience?
