You’ve probably seen it: a large central cross with four smaller crosses around it.

Often called the **Jerusalem Cross**.

People sometimes ask, “Is that biblical?”

Here’s the best answer:

The Bible doesn’t mandate that symbol—  
but it does mandate what the symbol is meant to represent.

## What the symbol points toward
The Jerusalem Cross is commonly associated with:
- the proclamation of Christ
- the spread of the gospel outward
- mission beyond one city, one nation, one people

Whether or not someone uses the symbol, the biblical emphasis is clear:

The message of Jesus is not meant to stay private.

## The biblical mission is outward
You can summarize a major New Testament trajectory as:

- Christ is proclaimed  
- disciples are formed  
- the Word spreads  
- the nations are invited  
- the Kingdom advances

A cross at the center with four around it is a visual way to say:

> Christ at the center, witness spreading outward.

## The warning: symbols can’t replace obedience
Any symbol becomes dangerous when it becomes a substitute for discipleship.

The Bible is not impressed by the display.

The Bible is formed by:
- truth
- repentance
- obedience
- love
- endurance

Use symbols if they help you remember mission.

Never use symbols to avoid mission.
