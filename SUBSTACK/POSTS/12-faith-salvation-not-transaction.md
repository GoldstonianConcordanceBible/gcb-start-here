Modern culture trains us to think in transactions:
- pay → receive
- click → download
- sign → access

But Scripture speaks of salvation as something deeper:

**rescue into covenant life.**

## Salvation is deliverance, not a product
Biblical salvation is God:
- bringing people out of bondage
- cleansing them
- forming them as a people
- teaching them to walk in His ways
- keeping His promise through Messiah
- securing their future in new creation

That’s not a receipt.

That’s a re-creation.

## Faith is trust that leads to following
Faith is not merely agreement.

Faith is covenant trust:
- trust that clings to God’s word
- trust that produces obedience
- trust that endures when life is hard

Obedience does not purchase salvation.

Obedience is what salvation begins to produce.

## Why this matters
If salvation is treated as a transaction:
- people expect instant comfort
- discipleship becomes optional
- holiness becomes “extra credit”

If salvation is covenant life:
- repentance becomes normal
- obedience becomes expected
- growth becomes inevitable
- endurance becomes possible

## A practical self-check
Ask yourself:

Am I treating God like a vendor…  
or like the covenant Lord?

That one question can correct a lifetime of drift.
