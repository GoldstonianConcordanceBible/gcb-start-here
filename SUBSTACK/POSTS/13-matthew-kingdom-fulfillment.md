Many people read Matthew as “Jesus’ moral teachings.”

Matthew is more than that.

Matthew is a **Kingdom document** written to show that Jesus is the promised Messiah and the rightful King.

## What Matthew is doing
Matthew repeatedly connects Jesus to:
- promise fulfillment
- covenant patterns
- prophetic expectation
- Israel’s storyline reaching its intended center

This is why Matthew feels “linked” to the Old Testament so often.

It’s not filler.

It’s proof of continuity.

## How to read Matthew without losing the plot
Use three lenses:

1) **Fulfillment lens:** What is being fulfilled here?  
2) **Kingdom lens:** What kind of King is Jesus revealing Himself to be?  
3) **Discipleship lens:** What does Kingdom life require of followers?

## The Sermon on the Mount isn’t “be nicer”
It’s not a self-improvement speech.

It’s Kingdom ethics:
- heart-level truth
- integrity without performance
- obedience without hypocrisy
- righteousness without show

Matthew isn’t saying: “Try harder.”

Matthew is saying: “Follow the King and become a citizen of His Kingdom.”
