Mark’s Gospel feels like action.

That’s intentional.

Mark is not writing to entertain.

Mark is pressing a question:

**Who is Jesus—and will you follow Him when it costs you?**

## Mark’s central tension
Mark shows:
- power and authority
- compassion and deliverance
- confrontation with evil
- misunderstanding by crowds
- failure by disciples

And then it narrows toward the cross.

Mark refuses a comfortable Christianity.

## The turning point: “Take up your cross”
Mark does not let discipleship remain theoretical.

Following Jesus includes:
- surrender
- truth
- endurance
- refusal to perform for approval

The cross is not only what Jesus does for us.

It becomes the shape of how we follow Him.

## Practical takeaway
Mark invites a hard self-check:

Do I want Jesus for comfort…  
or do I want Jesus as Lord?

Mark answers with the same clarity every time:

**Follow. Even when it’s costly.**
